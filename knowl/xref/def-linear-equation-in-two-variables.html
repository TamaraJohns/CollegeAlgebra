Definition 1.1.1.

Linear Equation in Two Variables is the equation of the form:
\begin{equation*} Ax+By=C \end{equation*}
where \(A,B,C\) are real numbers. Note that \(A\) and \(B\) cannot be both \(0\) simultaneously. Either\(A\) can be \(0\) or \(B\) can be \(0\text{,}\) but not both at the same.(See what happens if we plug in zeros for both \(A\) and \(B\text{.}\))
A quick reminder: the solution to a linear equation in two variables is an ordered pair that makes the equation true.
Every linear equation has infinitely many solutions, forming a straight line on a plane. In other words, the graph of any linear equation in two variables is always a straight line.
